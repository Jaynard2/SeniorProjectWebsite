Volunteer

  • Boots on the Ground
    • A service that my Dad started at church that helps do anything from mundane housework to simple construction (such as ramps) for anyone in need, especially widows and the elderly. I’ve spent many hours working with other church members, especially David Cress. It’s truly some of the most enjoyable volunteer work I’ve ever done.
  • Hannah
    • Hannah is a 94-year-old widow that is still living by herself and driving around town. Despite such surprising tenacity from such an elderly woman, she still needs some help around the house. I do some small jobs around the house that she can no longer do, from weed-whacking to even fixing her toilet.
  • Snow Shoveling
    • My neighborhood is full of retired people. The type of retired people that forgot that they are no longer 25, and so they try to shovel out their long driveways by themselves. To prevent injury, my brother and I have taken to shoveling out their driveways (at least 6) for them. After heavy snows, we also drive up to the church to clear out the entrances, too.
  • Elementary Robotics
    • One of the local elementary schools, Mount Washington Elementary, started a robotics program just last year. The teacher asked me to help teach both her and the students how to program EV3s.
  • 5th Grade Tutor
    • Julie Adams, the Instructional Coach at Mount Washington, asked several band members to start helping with some of the 5th graders that were having disciplinarian issues in class. Our job was to instruct the kids in how to behave respectfully both in and out of the classroom, and to be a role model for such behavior.
  • Community Ministries
    • My church participates with a food bank in Louisville to help distribute food to the hungry. When time allows, my Dad and I get food from Dare to Care and take it to our local pantry at the church. We then help to distribute it to those in need.

Extracurricular

  • Bullitt East Marching Band
    • I’ve participated in this band for the last 5 years, playing lead trumpet for 4 of them. We rehearse anywhere from 9 to 40 hours a week, although we average at about 15. Last year, I became Section Leader. This means that I help new students in my section learn everything there is to know about marching band, make decisions about how the section is going to both look and behave in public, as well as discipline those that behave inappropriately (Mr. Ervin, the band director, does most of it; I just do the little things). Mostly, though, the job is making sure everyone knows what they’re supposed to be doing. I also helped to start the Leadership Team this year. The Leadership Team is a collection of all the Section Leaders as well as some older members of the band. It helps to make decisions on the direction the band is going.
  • BEHS Robotics
    • My sophomore year, I helped to start the BEHS robotics team. We did decently at several competitions, and I became a better programmer and learned how to work with people that didn’t always put forth their best effort. The next year, I became the head programmer, and I helped several others code their robots and I started a library to help simplify programming them. Due to lack of funding, we never competed. This year, sadly, there is no robotics team as the teacher that led it has gotten a new job.
  • BETA Club
    • BETA Club is a club for intelligent, hardworking, students. It requires students take on leadership positions in the community as well as to do several hours of community service.
  • NHS
    • National Honors Society is a club very similar to BETA, with the largest difference being that it is only for upperclassmen.
