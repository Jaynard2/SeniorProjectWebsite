 Sophomore 
2016-2017

Literacy Growth Reflection

My tone on the trumpet grew so much between freshman and sophmore year - the difference is absolutely astounding. All though, I still had a very harsh tounge that often distrorted the tone and made playing quickly more difficult. My best writing piece, in my opinion, is "The French Reign of Terror: Was it Justified?", as it ties together several arguments created from different sources and stitches them together into a cohesive whole. I clearly had grown in my ability to explain a devoloped argument. I really needed to work on sentence structure and flow, though.

Personal Growth Reflection

The most difficult thing I had to deal with is rather suprising, and sadly depressing: a terrible teacher. One of the teachers I had (I will not name who) tried his hardest to fail conservative Christians. He would teach any veiwpoint outside of Christianity, espicially if it was actually hostile torwards it, with great glee; he only taught Christian "dogma" (his words) when he absolutely had to, and even then with obvious displeasure. He would teach liberal world views (such as socialism) as if it was the very Word of God, and any conservative view points treated with scorn. He would tell any student that disagreed with him that they were ignorrant, no matter how well reasoned their argument or solid their facts. I disagreed with him a lot.
 Although he was a terrible teacher, and I despised his classroom, I did learn a very valuable lesson: preserverence. I could have simply given up in his class, and either gone with his lies or just let him fail me, but I didn't. I pushed through his class, scraped by with a B, and held on to my beliefs.
