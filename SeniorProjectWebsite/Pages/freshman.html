 Freshman

Literacy Growth Reflection

As a freshman, it appears that I often used puctuation in strange, if not completey incorrect, ways. It also appears that I rarely proof-read what I wrote, as their are numerous typos. My tone on the trumpet was also rather poor; to think I thought myself amazing! I think that the gun control speech was my best piece, although there are several things I would change about it now. It shows that, even as a freshman, I was capable of forming a at least semi-complex argument backed with strong, factual evidence. I did need to work on explaining my argument, though.

Personal Growth Reflection

I grew a lot as a freshman, and in more than just height. Comming out of middle school, I knew that I wanted to be in the engineering feild, but not much more than that. By the end of winter break, that changed. For Christmas, I had recieved a book to teach myself how to program (Javascript Novice to Ninja, by Darren Jones), and I began to teach myself how to program. It is now one of my favorite hobbies, and I plan to major in Computer Engineering.
